~8 Objects To Throw Around Room~

By StarsInDust

3/2/2026

 

 

Create your images

Open PhotoShop

Turn on Your Grid

Open your image, and then set up your guides in Photoshop.

Photoshop-Edit-Preferences- Guides, Grid, & Slices

 

Set it to 32px

Grid needs to be turned on.

View -Show-Grid

Shrink your elements down to fit into the grid space.

Duplicate the layers into their own images. You can open a new image, set the size to 32px X 32px, and then slide each of these into it. Rememeber to save each image individually.

Save to Game Maker Sprite Folder

Now save each individual picture to your sprite folder inside of Game Maker

C:\Users\YOUR_USER_NAME\GameMakerProjects\My First Game Project

 

Note-We Use sprites with objects, not tile maps, for these throw around objects. It is easier to individually code each of our objects that way.

 

Create Your Sprites

Just bringing your images into the Game Makers sprite folder does not add a sprite to the game. You must go through the process of actually creating the Sprite in Game Maker. This is because the app will be doing some other stuff behind the scene, so that your sprite will work.

You will want to do this process for each one of your sprites.

Right click on the Sprite folder. Select -Create -Sprite.

sprite_heart

sprite_apple

sprite_bomb

 

With your new sprite created in the Asset Browser, Go to the Middle of your workspace. Import the correct image of the sprite. Don’t worry about the size of the sprite that is showing in your workspace, before you add the image. As soon as it grabs your sprite, (if you had set the size to 32px) the app will pick up that size, and show it correctly.

Now do the same thing for your other two elements. The apple, and the bomb.

With all of your sprites in there you can now create your objects

Create Your Objects

Right click on the Objects folder, and then create each one of your objects. Use the appropriate sprite for the face of each object.

Do the Same thing, creating the object for your other two sprites, and give them the appropriate face.
